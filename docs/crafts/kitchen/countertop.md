# 厨房台面工艺 — Kitchen Countertop Fabrication & Installation

## 1. Overview

Kitchen countertops are one of the most heavily used surfaces in a home. This guide covers material selection, edge profiling, seam treatment, sink cutouts, installation procedures, and long-term maintenance for residential kitchen countertops.

---

## 2. Material Comparison

### 2.1 Quartz Stone (石英石)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Composition | ~90-95% crushed natural quartz + 5-10% polymer resin |
| Thickness | 15 mm, 20 mm (standard), 30 mm (premium) |
| Hardness | Mohs 6.5-7 |
| Heat Resistance | Moderate — up to 150°C; use trivets |
| Stain Resistance | Excellent; non-porous surface |
| Price Range | ¥600 - ¥1,800 / m² |
| Lifespan | 15-25 years |

**Advantages:** Non-porous, wide color range, consistent pattern, low maintenance, antibacterial.

**Disadvantages:** Visible seams, can chip at edges, not suitable for direct high-heat contact.

### 2.2 Granite (花岗岩)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Composition | 100% natural igneous rock |
| Thickness | 20 mm, 30 mm |
| Hardness | Mohs 6-7 |
| Heat Resistance | Excellent — withstands direct hot pans |
| Stain Resistance | Good when sealed; porous without sealing |
| Price Range | ¥800 - ¥3,000+ / m² |
| Lifespan | 25+ years (effectively permanent) |

**Advantages:** Unique natural patterns, high heat tolerance, adds property value.

**Disadvantages:** Requires periodic sealing (every 1-2 years), natural fissures may propagate, heavy (requires sturdy cabinetry).

### 2.3 Stainless Steel (不锈钢)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Composition | 304-grade food-safe stainless steel |
| Thickness | 0.8 mm (cladding) to 1.5 mm (solid top) |
| Hardness | N/A — metal surface |
| Heat Resistance | Excellent |
| Stain Resistance | Excellent; hygienic surface |
| Price Range | ¥1,000 - ¥2,500 / m² |
| Lifespan | 20+ years |

**Advantages:** Seamless integration with sink, completely hygienic, heatproof, modern aesthetic.

**Disadvantages:** Shows scratches and fingerprints easily, can dent, noisy (pans clank), limited color options.

### 2.4 Quick Selection Guide

| Priority | Recommended Material |
|----------|---------------------|
| Budget-conscious | Quartz (mid-range) |
| Low maintenance | Quartz or Stainless Steel |
| Cooking-heavy kitchen | Granite (heat) or Stainless Steel |
| Luxury / resale value | Granite (premium slabs) |
| Hygiene-critical (commercial style) | Stainless Steel |
| Consistent aesthetic | Quartz |

## 3. Edge Profiles

| Profile | Description | Best For | Cost Impact |
|---------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| Straight (直边) | Flat 90-degree edge | Modern, minimal | Baseline |
| Beveled (斜边) | Angled cut at 45 degrees | Transitional | +5-10% |
| Bullnose (圆边) | Fully rounded edge | Family homes, safety | +10-15% |
| Half Bullnose (半圆边) | Rounded top, flat bottom | Balanced look | +8-12% |
| Ogee (S形边) | Decorative S-curve | Traditional, luxury | +20-30% |
| Waterfall (瀑布边) | Vertical drop to floor | Island features | +30-50% |
| Eased (微圆边) | Slightly rounded corners | Everyday use | Baseline +5% |

**Installation Note:** Edge profiles must be specified before fabrication. Post-fabrication edge changes require re-polishing the entire slab face and are rarely cost-effective.

## 4. Seam Treatment (拼接缝处理)

### 4.1 Seam Placement Principles

1. **Avoid** placing seams directly in front of the sink or cooktop.
2. **Minimize** the number of seams — ideally one per continuous run.
3. **Locate** seams at natural break points (e.g., at a corner or between cabinet sections).
4. **Never** place a seam within 150 mm of a sink cutout edge.

### 4.2 Seam Execution Steps

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Dry-fit both pieces; verify alignment and level |
| 2 | Apply color-matched epoxy adhesive to both edges using a notched spreader |
| 3 | Clamp pieces together using seam clamps; align within 0.5 mm tolerance |
| 4 | Remove excess adhesive immediately with a razor blade before curing |
| 5 | Allow adhesive to cure per manufacturer specification (typically 24 hours) |
| 6 | Polish seam area with progressively finer diamond pads (50, 100, 200, 400 grit) |
| 7 | Final seal (if required by material) |

### 4.3 Acceptable Seam Width

| Material | Max Seam Width |
|----------|---------------|
| Quartz | 0.5 mm (barely visible) |
| Granite | 0.5-1.0 mm (depends on pattern) |
| Stainless Steel | Welded — no visible seam |

## 5. Sink Cutout (水槽开孔)

### 5.1 Cutout Types

| Type | Description | Pros | Cons |
|------|-------------|------|------|
| Top-mount / Drop-in (台上盆) | Sink rests on counter lip | Easy installation, replaceable | Rim collects debris, visible edge |
| Undermount (台下盆) | Sink mounted beneath counter | Clean counter wipe, seamless | More expensive install, weight concern |
| Integrated (一体盆) | Counter and sink fabricated as one piece | Zero seams, hygienic | Expensive, limited styles |

### 5.2 Cutout Dimensions

| Sink Size | Recommended Cutout (mm) | Min Edge Margin (mm) |
|-----------|------------------------|---------------------|
| Single bowl 600 mm | 560 x 420 | 60 |
| Double bowl 800 mm | 760 x 420 | 80 |
| Farmhouse apron | Custom per model | 40 (front), 60 (sides/back) |
| Bar prep 380 mm | 340 x 340 | 60 |

**Critical:** Always use the manufacturer's template or the actual sink for tracing. Subtract 3-5 mm from the template for undermount installations to account for the mounting rim.

### 5.3 Cutout Execution

1. Place template on underside of countertop; trace with pencil.
2. Drill four 10 mm starter holes just inside the traced corners.
3. Use a jigsaw with a diamond or carbide blade to cut along the line.
4. Sand cut edges with 200-grit diamond pad.
5. For undermount: install clips or epoxy brackets per sink manufacturer.
6. Apply a bead of food-safe silicone caulk between sink rim and countertop.
7. Allow silicone to cure 24 hours before connecting plumbing.

## 6. Installation Process

### 6.1 Pre-Installation Checklist

- [ ] Cabinetry installed, leveled, and secured
- [ ] Cabinet top rails or plywood subtop installed (minimum 18 mm plywood)
- [ ] Plumbing rough-in complete and pressure-tested
- [ ] Electrical outlets positioned per appliance plan
- [ ] Countertop template verified against site measurements
- [ ] Slabs inspected for cracks, chips, or color mismatches before cutting
- [ ] Access path from truck to kitchen is clear (protect floors and doorways)
- [ ] All necessary tools on site: shims, silicone, clamps, level, caulk gun

### 6.2 Installation Steps

| Step | Task | Detail |
|------|------|--------|
| 1 | **Dry fit** | Place all pieces without adhesive; verify fit, alignment, and overhang |
| 2 | **Level cabinets** | Shim as needed; ensure all tops are within 1 mm of level across the full run |
| 3 | **Apply adhesive** | Run 6 mm beads of silicone or construction adhesive along cabinet top rails in a zigzag pattern |
| 4 | **Set slabs** | Lower slabs into position using suction cups (for heavy materials); press firmly |
| 5 | **Join seams** | Follow seam treatment procedure (Section 4.2) |
| 6 | **Verify level** | Check with 2 m straightedge; maximum deviation 1 mm over 2 m |
| 7 | **Install backsplash** | Adhere with silicone; caulk top edge |
| 8 | **Cut faucet hole** | Use hole saw; diameter per faucet spec (typically 32-35 mm) |
| 9 | **Caulk perimeter** | Apply color-matched silicone along wall and backsplash joints |
| 10 | **Final clean** | Remove all protective film, adhesive residue, and dust |

### 6.3 Overhang Specifications

| Application | Max Overhang (unsupported) | Support Required Beyond |
|-------------|---------------------------|------------------------|
| Quartz (20 mm) | 300 mm | 300 mm — install corbels or brackets |
| Granite (30 mm) | 350 mm | 350 mm — install corbels or brackets |
| Breakfast bar (bar seating) | 400-450 mm | Steel brackets or cantilever frame |
| Island waterfall | N/A | Full-height cabinet or steel subframe |

## 7. Maintenance Guide

### 7.1 Daily Care

| Material | Cleaning Method | Avoid |
|----------|----------------|-------|
| Quartz | Warm water + mild dish soap, soft cloth | Abrasive pads, bleach, oven cleaner |
| Granite | Stone-specific pH-neutral cleaner | Vinegar, lemon juice, ammonia |
| Stainless Steel | Stainless steel cleaner, microfiber cloth | Steel wool, chlorine bleach |

### 7.2 Periodic Maintenance Schedule

| Interval | Action | Material |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Monthly | Inspect seams and caulk lines; re-caulk if lifting | All |
| Every 6 months | Test granite seal: drop water — if it absorbs, reseal | Granite |
| Annually | Reseal granite with penetrating sealer | Granite |
| As needed | Polish out minor scratches with stone polishing compound | Granite |
| As needed | Remove mineral deposits with stone-safe descaler | All |

### 7.3 Damage Repair Guide

| Issue | Repair Method |
|-------|--------------|
| Small chip (quartz/granite) | Fill with color-matched epoxy; sand flush; polish |
| Crack (granite) | Professional repair with epoxy resin and reinforcement pins |
| Scratch (stainless steel) | Polish with grain using abrasive pad (320-400 grit); follow grain direction |
| Burn mark (quartz) | Professional resurfacing; may require section replacement |
| Stain (granite) | Poultice: baking soda + water paste, cover with plastic 24 hours, remove |

## 8. Quality Acceptance Criteria

| Item | Standard |
|------|----------|
| Levelness | ≤ 1 mm deviation over 2 m |
| Seam visibility | Invisible at 600 mm viewing distance (quartz) |
| Overhang tolerance | ±2 mm from specified dimension |
| Cutout edge | Smooth, no chips or roughness |
| Caulk lines | Uniform width, no gaps or breaks |
| Surface | No scratches, chips, or etching visible under normal lighting |
